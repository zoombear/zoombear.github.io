Botanical collections in different localities.

Expeditions to northeastern and central Mexico Oct. 1922 -- Jan. 1923 inclusive
With E.L. Furlong, Curator of Paleontology, University of California Berkeley. Botanical collecting trip on slopes of Mt. Popocatepetl.

Botanical collecting trip to western Mexico; State of Sinaloa:
Sept. 15 to Dec. 11, 1925
With Mrs. Roxanna Ferris, Curator of Herbarium, Stanford University.
500 Nos; 3,500 specimens

Botanical collecting trip to western Mexico; States of Sinaloa, Nayarit, Jalisco.
Sept. 9, 1926 -- May 25, 1927 inclusive. Sea level to 6,000 foot elevation in Sierra Madre.
20 sets of about 12,000 specimens, distributed to 20 major herbaria in United States and abroad.
over 1,600 nos.; 33,000 specimens

Botanical collecting trip to Alaska; Mt. McKinley National Park
June 9 -- Sept. 13, 1928 inclusive. First general collection made of the Park flora.
17 sets of about 4,500 specimens distributed to 17 major herbaria in United States and Europe.
365 nos.; over 6,100 specimens

Expedition, Paleontological and Botanical, to northern and central Mexico:
May 6 through Sept 1, 1929. (Left SF Apr 26, arr. SF Sept 8)
With E.L. Furlong, Curator of Paleontology, California Institute of Technology, Pasadena, California.
16 sets of about 3,000 specimens, distributed through United States and abroad. 315 nos.; over 5,000 specimens

Botanical Expedition to Brazil and Peru
Nov. 15, 1929 -- Feb 10, 1932 inclusive (Left SF Oct 16, 1929; Arr SF Sept 8, 1932)
With Mrs. Agnes Chase (for a short time), Agrostology Dept., U.S. Dept. of Agriculture, Washington, D.C.
Rio de Janeiro; Vicosa and Diamantina in State of Minas Geraes, Brazil. Amazon and other rivers in States of Para and Amazonas, Brazil. 3,000 miles to Upper Amazon and Santiago Rivers in Dept. of Loreto, in Trans-Andean Peru.
24 sets of about 60,000 specimens distributed to major herbaria in United States and Europe.
3,200 nos 65,000 specimens.

Botanical Expedition to South America (1934-37): (Left SF Sept 6, 1934; Arr. SF Feb 5, 1937):

Sept. 1934 - Sept. 1935 inclusive. Ecuador, U.S. Dept. of Agriculture, Bureau of Plant Introduction and Exploration Appointment, to search for Palms, Cinchonas, Soil-binding plants and general collections. In Ecuador and Colombia.

Provincias Los Rios, Leon, Guayas, Chimborazo, Tungurahua, Pichincha, Napo-Pastaza, Imbabura, Carchi and in Colombia: Departamento Narino. October 18, 1934 to September 19, 1935 inclusive. 900 numbers, 5,000 specimens.

University of California Botanical Garden Expedition to the Andes (Dr. Goodspeed): October 15, 1935 to February 1936 inclusive. Peru: Departamentos Lima, Huanuco, Arequipa, Puno. Bolivia: Departamento La Paz. Argentina: Provincias Jujuy, Tucuman, Cordoba, San Luis, Mendoza, Las Heras. Chile: Provincia Aconcagua, Curico. 300 numbers, 1,900 specimens.

Collecting For Herself in South America, October 20, 1935 to December 25, 1936. Peru: Departamentos Lima, Huanuco, Arequipa, Puno, Cuzco. Bolivia: Departamento La Paz. Argentina: Provincias Jujuy, Tucuman, Mendoza, Las Heras, Tierra del Fuego. Chile: Provincias Aconcagua, Curico, Chiloe, Magallanes, Valdivia. Ecuador: Esmeraldas (and a few near the Colombian border). 1,000 numbers, 13,000 specimens.
